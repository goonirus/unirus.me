Simulating Supply Lines:
A Logistical Nightmare

I've been banging my head against the wall for the past week trying to make "starvation" fun.

I'm working on a Napoleonic prototype where logistics should be the main enemy. Historically, more soldiers died from disease and hunger than from cannonballs. But translating that into a game mechanic is incredibly frustrating.

Current Iteration:
Players have to trace a path of hexes back to a Supply Depot. If the path is blocked by enemy ZOC (Zone of Control), units take attrition damage.

The problem is the bookkeeping. Checking supply status for 50 individual units every turn is boring. It slows the game down to a crawl.

I tried simplifying it: "If you are in enemy territory, lose 1 Supply Token per turn." But then it feels too abstract. It loses the feeling of being cut off.

Idea for next playtest: What if supply isn't a state, but an action? You have to spend Command Points to "Push Supply". If you don't push, your frontline freezes. This makes it an active choice rather than a passive punishment.

Will try this on Tuesday. Hopefully, it doesn't result in another spreadsheet simulator.
