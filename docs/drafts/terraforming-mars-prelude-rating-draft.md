The user accepted the following calibration after those corrections:

- Quality tier: `S+ / 卓越扩展，档内顶尖`.
- Enable recommendation: `常驻，从第一局开始即可加入`.
- Integration is extremely high.
- Prelude accelerates the opening, creates setup variation, establishes an early direction, and improves replayability.
- A new player choosing Prelude cards imperfectly is a strategy-skill issue, not meaningful additional learning burden.
- The base game's official opening without a complete draft already trades some balance for speed. Prelude adds only a small increment to that existing variance and should not be deducted for a problem it did not create.
- The S+ tier is not justified by saying the base game feels incomplete without Prelude. Necessity and expansion quality remain separate judgments.
- Prelude controls acceleration with unusual precision: it removes slow buildup without replacing the base game's later development arc.
- Very few later designs have reproduced such a large tempo improvement through such a small mechanism, and similar attempts rarely match its balance of impact and restraint.
- Most Prelude cards sit close to a roughly `21 M€` value model. Their delivery differs, but the expansion's numerical baseline is stable and its improvement is not purchased by abandoning balance.

## Question Audit

No mandatory follow-up question currently remains. Do not reopen the following judgments unless the user introduces a conflicting view or new evidence:

- The expansion's net gain is already established: it accelerates the opening, creates meaningful initial variation, and improves replayability.
- The cost of that acceleration is already bounded by the `S+` tier and the recommendation to include it from the first play. Do not re-ask whether Prelude removes a valuable opening phase merely because that is a common external criticism.
- Opening direction is already treated as a benefit. There is no current evidence that route locking materially weakens the expansion, so do not invent that caveat as a compulsory question.
- Opening balance and corporation-card combinations are already covered at the level relevant to this review: the base game inherits most of the no-draft variance, while Prelude adds only a limited increment.
- Player-count differences, solo play, and the secondary corporations/project cards have not been identified as verdict-changing issues. They are optional topics, not omissions that must be converted into questions.

Before any later follow-up, compare the proposed question against this audit and the confirmed judgments above. Ask only if the answer could alter the tier, recommendation, or substantive public analysis.

## Scope And Structure

- The page evaluates the original 2018 `Prelude`, not `Prelude 2` or later promotional Prelude cards.
- It uses the expansion framework's quality tier and enable recommendation instead of inventing a base-game-style weighted matrix.
- It is linked from the `Terraforming Mars` review and deliberately omitted from the global all-games table.
- Price is excluded under the global rating method and therefore does not need to be restated as game-specific prose.
