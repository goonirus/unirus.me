### Claims To Preserve

- The borrowed or familiar mechanism is used exceptionally well.
- X tokens are necessary rather than decorative. They greatly expand the player's operational range without adding much learning cost, producing a strong `Depth/Complex` relationship: low complexity buys high depth.
- The value of X tokens becomes especially visible in the final few turns, when small changes in action strength and timing determine whether a plan can be completed.
- Moving an Action card to slot 1 solely to gain an X token appears highly inefficient, but is an essential pressure-release option. It permits a small immediate loss to avoid a much larger future loss.
- The upgrade system is precisely calibrated in three separate ways: what each upgrade does, how upgrades are obtained, and the cap on the number of upgrades.
- The system is not only theoretically elegant; it has also produced very good play results in the user's experience.

### Public-Prose Direction

The five-card system should be presented as more than a reused action-selection device. `Ark Nova` improves its practical resilience through X tokens and tightly limited upgrades. These additions preserve a simple rule surface while creating substantial late-game control, making this mechanism one of the clearest examples of the game's ability to obtain high depth from low complexity.

## Card System, Time Structure, And Balance

>
> 而方舟跟火星有根本上的区别，即回合制度和手牌上限，这两者决定了玩法截然不同。回合制度是更为根本的差异。火星对回合没有限制，我一个时代就能运行三十个回合，玩家直接比的是资源；而动物园回合数就是决定成败的关键，30个回合基本已经是玩的一般的局了，玩家直接比的是资源转化效率。手牌上限也是关键的一点，动物园是有严格的手牌上限的，所以动物园考验的是玩家的短期规划能力，而火星则更考验长期规划。有些喜欢“藏牌”火星高手，在最后一个回合完成引擎构筑一回合爆一百分，但是玩动物园就玩的捉襟见肘，坐如针毡，就是这个原因（比如我）。
>
> 这两者机制的根本不同，甚至在平衡性上也产生了影响，这也是动物园设计高明的地方。如果我们做数值分析的话，我们会发现火星的标和数值都是非常精妙的，数值模型搭建的非常完善，因此其可延展性特别强，出的扩和promo卡基本都是让游戏更加进步的，本来较弱的电资源也是借着殖民地扩完成了翻身。除了一些特别超模的卡以外，和个别几张特别垃圾的卡（地下爆破），大多数卡都是在一个平稳的区间内。反观动物园，标更加简单，效果也更为直白，在如此好算的数值模型之下，卡牌的平衡性做的却实在不怎么样，超模的卡能赚接近20块，就算我们不考虑所有效果牌，或许在发售之前不是很好预估（果断等），以及难以打出的卡牌（大熊猫、王鹫等），也有两只老虎这种很容易打出且白值就超模十几块的，我很难说这是一个优秀的数值模型。
>
> 然而动物园的短循环很大程度上弥补了这一点，短循环的核心在于节奏，卡牌的优劣就不这么重要了。在火星中，你开局买下了强大的反重力引擎，如果能打出基本上就能回本，就算打不出也就是亏两块钱（虽然前期的三块钱和后期的一块钱并不能简单的算作差两块）；而动物园中，如果你开局拿了大熊猫一局都没有拿到熊标，那相当于你全局手牌上限-1而且非常的卡节奏，或许不如在开局老老实实的买一只可爱的普通壁蜥。
>
> 另外就是公共排列，动物园获取的卡牌不是非常的依赖盲抽，这也一定程度上让数值问题不这么严重。
>
> 当然动物园里还是有些完全不会卡手而且赚模的卡牌，以及有一些几乎碾压的卡牌，所以我只能说是“很大程度弥补”，数值设计的缺陷依然是不可忽视的。另外设计上的失败的卡牌也是比数值更严重的问题，有些动物因为就业空间太有限而人见人嫌（鸸鹋：我也没有很亏模啊）。

### Claims To Preserve

#### Shared Card Language With Terraforming Mars

- Animal cards resemble the immediate-return role of green cards in `Terraforming Mars`.
- Sponsor cards resemble the long-term-return role of blue cards.
- Conservation Project cards resemble awards that a player can introduce personally.
- Both games rely heavily on tags and prerequisites.
- `Ark Nova` has a rich deck, and its individual card designs are generally good.

#### Fundamental Difference: Time Before Cards

- The two fundamental differences are the turn structure and hand limit; the turn structure is the deeper distinction.
- `Terraforming Mars` places no comparable limit on the number of actions within a generation. A player can take an extremely long generation, so players compete more directly through accumulated resources.
- In `Ark Nova`, the number of turns is itself decisive. Roughly 30 turns already indicates an ordinary rather than strong game, and players compete more directly through resource-conversion efficiency.
- The strict hand limit reinforces this distinction. `Ark Nova` tests short-term planning, while `Terraforming Mars` places more emphasis on long-term planning.
- A skilled `Terraforming Mars` player may hold cards, finish an engine in the final generation, and convert it into an explosive scoring turn. The same habit can make that player feel severely constrained in `Ark Nova`; the user identifies personally with this example.

#### Numerical Balance

- The different time structures alter the practical consequences of card imbalance. This mitigation is part of `Ark Nova`'s design intelligence, but it does not make the underlying numerical model excellent.
- `Terraforming Mars` has an intricate tag and value model with strong extensibility. Expansions and promotional cards have generally developed the system; for example, `Colonies` adds a three-energy trading threshold that connects energy production to the existing energy-to-heat cycle.
- Aside from some highly overpowered cards and a few exceptionally weak cards such as `Underground Detonations`, most `Terraforming Mars` cards remain within a stable value range.
- `Ark Nova` uses simpler tags and more direct effects, which should make its values easier to model, yet its card balance is substantially weaker.
- Some overpowered cards can exceed their expected value by almost 20 money.
- Even after setting aside effects that may have been hard to estimate before publication, such as `Determination`, and difficult-to-play cards such as Giant Panda and King Vulture, easy-to-play examples such as the two tigers can exceed the expected printed value by more than 10 money.
- Difficulty estimating an effect such as `Determination` before publication is only a charitable explanation from the developer's perspective. It does not excuse the result; fundamentally, the miss still reflects insufficient development work.
- For that reason, the user cannot describe `Ark Nova` as having an excellent numerical model.

#### Why The Game Still Works

- `Ark Nova`'s short loop substantially mitigates its uneven card values because tempo, rather than raw card value, is the central concern.
- In `Terraforming Mars`, buying `Anti-Gravity Technology` at the start is close to self-correcting: playing it can repay the investment, while never playing it costs only the purchase price difference. This arithmetic still needs the user's important caveat that three early money and one late money are not directly comparable.
- In `Ark Nova`, holding Giant Panda without ever finding a bear tag effectively reduces the hand limit by one for the entire game and damages tempo. A modest, immediately playable animal such as a common wall lizard may therefore be the better opening card.
- The public display reduces dependence on blind draws and further limits the damage caused by uneven card values.
- This is only substantial mitigation, not a complete solution. Some cards are both easy to use and above the expected value curve, and some are close to overwhelming; the numerical weakness remains material.
- A failed functional design is more serious than a mere numerical miss. Some animals are disliked because their viable use cases are too narrow even when their nominal value is not especially poor; Emu is the user's example.

### Structural Direction For The Public Article

Keep the causal order intact:

1. Establish the close card-language comparison with `Terraforming Mars`.
2. Explain that turn structure, followed by hand limit, produces a fundamentally different game.
3. Use that difference to explain short-term versus long-term planning and resource-conversion efficiency.
4. Only then compare the two numerical models.
5. Explain how tempo and the public display mitigate `Ark Nova`'s weak card balance.
6. End with the scope limit: mitigation does not erase overpowered cards or functionally unsuccessful designs.

Do not flatten this into a generic statement that randomness creates replayability. The main argument concerns how time structure changes the practical value and danger of cards.

### Fact Check: The Scoring Change

- Confirmed. The original rules calculated victory points as the zoo's Appeal minus the target Appeal value associated with the player's position on the Conservation track. Scores could therefore be positive or negative, and the two markers meeting corresponded to 0 points.
- An official 2023 rules update changed the presentation. Current printings place a small white scoring value on each Conservation-track space; players add that number to their Appeal. The markers meeting now corresponds to 100 points.
- The new result is mathematically the old result plus 100. It does not change ranking, balance, or strategy; it changes how progress and the final result are presented to players.
- The user's judgment about the experiential effect is suitable for the article: changing a result around zero into one around 100 makes the achievement feel more substantial. Keep this as the user's evaluation rather than presenting it as a measured universal response.

### Strategic Conclusion

> 双轨并不是要求玩家按照固定比例同步推进。魅力记录条在前期直接影响钱产，是启动动物园和维持行动效率的基础；保护记录条则通过行动卡升级、协会事务员和随机奖励等节点提供发展机会，并在中后期承担更高效的终局推进。玩家可以根据地图、手牌和保护项目明显偏向其中一条，但不能长期忽略另一条，真正的判断在于每一局应该何时从经济积累转向终局推进。
>
> 两轨相遇触发终局，也让计分机制同时成为一套收尾时机机制。主动越线并不意味着立刻锁定胜利，其他玩家通常仍有最后一个回合，因此玩家需要同时计算自己能一次跨过多远、对手还保留多少爆发空间，以及是否应该暂时停在终局线前。这种可以预判、蓄力和抢先触发的越线竞速，为游戏最后几个回合增加了很有价值的规划与博弈，也进一步强化了《方舟动物园》对短期资源转化效率的考验。

> 休息机制是《方舟动物园》交互的核心，它并不直接夺取其他玩家的资源，却把所有人的行动连接到同一条时间线上，对整局的对局节奏产生天翻地覆的影响。在多人局中尤其如此：休息轨道并不由任何一名玩家单独控制，只要其他玩家都有推进休息的动力，看似遥远的休息格也可能在自己再次行动前被迅速填满。因此，判断其他玩家接下来可能选择什么，与规划自己的行动卡同样重要。
